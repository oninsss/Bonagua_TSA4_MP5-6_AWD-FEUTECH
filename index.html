Spicy Soft Tofu | 순두부찌개

Spicy soft tofu stew is a popular Korean dish made with silky soft tofu, vegetables, seafood, and meat. It’s a hearty, spicy, savory, delicious dish that pretty much everyone loves. It’s also very nutritious and low in calories. It’s a great dish to enjoy all year round, but especially in the winter.

Ingredients

  • - Soft tofu
  • - Gochugaru (Korean red chili flakes)
  • - Gochujang (Korean red chili paste)
  • - Soy sauce
  • - Beef or seafood
  • - Vegetables (zucchini, mushrooms, onions, etc.)
  • - Eggs
  • - Water

Fun Facts

  • - Spicy soft tofu stew is a popular Korean dish that's enjoyed for its comforting and warming qualities.
  • - It's often served in a hot stone bowl (dolsot) to keep the stew hot and bubbling throughout the meal.
  • - The dish is known for its spicy, savory, and slightly sweet flavor, making it a favorite among spicy food enthusiasts.

Tteokbokki | 떡볶이

Tteokbokki is a popular Korean street food dish made with chewy rice cakes (tteok) cooked in a spicy, sweet, and savory sauce. The dish features cylindrical rice cakes simmered with gochujang (Korean red chili paste), soy sauce, sugar, and other seasonings. It often includes fish cakes (oden), boiled eggs, and scallions, providing a variety of textures and flavors.

Ingredients

  • - Rice cakes (tteok)
  • - Gochujang (Korean red chili paste)
  • - Soy sauce
  • - Sugar
  • - Fish cakes (oden)
  • - Boiled eggs
  • - Scallions (chopped)
  • - Water

Fun Facts

  • Tteokbokki is a popular street food in Korea, commonly sold at food stalls (pojangmacha) and enjoyed as a snack or light meal.
  • It has a distinct spicy and slightly sweet flavor, making it a favorite among spicy food enthusiasts.
  • Tteokbokki variations include additions like cheese, ramen noodles, and vegetables to create unique twists on the classic dish.

Bibimbop | 비빔밥

Bibimbap is a traditional Korean mixed rice dish that features a colorful assortment of vegetables, meat, and a fried egg on top of a bed of warm rice. The name "Bibimbap" literally translates to "mixed rice," as the dish is traditionally mixed together with a spicy gochujang (Korean red chili paste) sauce before eating. Bibimbap is known for its vibrant presentation and harmonious blend of flavors and textures.

Ingredients

  • - Cooked rice
  • - Gochujang (Korean red chili paste)
  • - Beef (bulgogi)
  • - Carrots (julienned)
  • - Spinach (blanched)
  • - Bean sprouts (blanched)
  • - Zucchini (julienned)
  • - Mushrooms (sliced)
  • - Eggs (fried)

    • Fun Facts

    • - Bibimbap is a popular Korean dish that's enjoyed for its vibrant colors, fresh flavors, and customizable nature.
    • - It's a versatile dish that can be made with a variety of ingredients, making it a great way to use up leftovers.
    • - Bibimbap is often served in a hot stone bowl (dolsot) to create a crispy layer of rice on the bottom, known as "nurungji."

Jajangmyeon | 자장면

Spicy soft tofu stew is a popular Korean dish made with silky soft tofu, vegetables, seafood, and meat. It’s a hearty, spicy, savory, delicious dish that pretty much everyone loves. It’s also very nutritious and low in calories. It’s a great dish to enjoy all year round, but especially in the winter.

Ingredients

  • - Chunjang (black bean paste)
  • - Pork or beef (diced)
  • - Onions (chopped)
  • - Zucchini (diced)
  • - Potatoes (diced)
  • - Cabbage (shredded)
  • - Cucumber (thinly sliced, for garnish)
  • - Pickled radish (side dish)

Fun Facts

  • - Jajangmyeon is often enjoyed as a comfort food in Korea, commonly ordered for delivery or takeout.
  • - It was adapted from the Chinese dish "Zha Jiang Mian" but has evolved into a unique Korean version.
  • - In Korea, there's a tradition of eating Jajangmyeon on "Black Day" (April 14th) for those who didn't receive gifts or chocolates on Valentine's Day or White Day.

Price: $10.00
